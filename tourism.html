Places to Visit

Kenya blends dramatic geography with unique ecosystems. These signature destinations showcase safaris, lakes, mountains and ocean life in one country.

Maasai Mara

Famous for the Great Migration, game drives, and golden plains rich with big cats and antelope.

Mount Kenya

A UNESCO World Heritage Site with alpine trails, glacier views and diverse ecological zones.

Diani Beach

Turquoise waters, coral reefs and palm-fringed sands make this coast perfect for rest and water sports.

Amboseli National Park

Renowned for close-up elephant sightings and iconic views of snow-capped Mount Kilimanjaro.

Lake Nakuru

A beautiful rift valley lake known for birdlife, rhinos, and dramatic escarpment landscapes.

Lamu Island

A historic Swahili destination with narrow alleys, dhow traditions and unique coastal heritage.

History

Kenya’s story stretches from ancient trade routes on the Swahili coast to modern nationhood, shaped by resilience, diversity and innovation.

Coastal Civilizations

For centuries, coastal cities such as Mombasa connected Africa, Arabia and Asia through trade and culture.

Independence Era

After anti-colonial struggles, Kenya gained independence in 1963 and began building a modern democratic state.

Contemporary Kenya

Today Kenya stands out for tech innovation, creative industries, conservation leadership and vibrant urban culture.
